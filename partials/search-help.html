Quick Search

Enter a single search term for kanji from one of the following four categories: 1) a kanji character, 2) an Onyomi reading in katakana, 3) a Kunyomi reading in hiragana, 4) a kanji's simplified English meaning.

Examples:

  • 親
  • シン
  • おや
  • parent

Note: In Quick Search mode Onyomi and Kunyomi readings may only be entered in Japanese. Please use the Advanced Search mode to search for kanji by reading in romaji.

Advanced Search

Enter one or more search terms in Japanese or romaji from all of the available categories in Kanji alive. Advanced search queries must be entered in the form key:value where key refers to the search category (e.g. Onyomi reading) and value refers to the search term for a given key (for example, on:shin or on:シン).

List of available Keys:

on: Onyomi reading, R/J (Katakana) on:shin or on:シン
kun: Kunyomi reading, R/J (Hiragana) kun:oya or kun:おや
kem: Kanji English meaning, E kem:parent
ks: Kanji stroke number, # ks:16
kanji: Kanji character, J kanji:親
rjn: Radical Japanese name, R/J (Hiragana) rjn:miru or rjn:みる
rem: Radical English meaning, E rem:see
rs: Radical stroke number, # rs:7
rpos: Radical Position, R/J (Hiragana) rpos:hen or rpos:へん
grade: Grade level, # grade:2
text: Textbook see below for details
list: Study List see below for details

In Advanced Search mode, two or more search terms may be combined by entering these one after the other separated by a space. The search results will show only those kanji common to all of the categories listed (for example, grade:3 rjn:ninben – all Grade 3 kanji with the radical "ninben"). Any number of search categories may be combined in this manner.

mosr: Mimi o sumaseba (reading only) text:mosr:c2
mnt: Majo no takkyuubin text:mnt:c1
mntr: Majo no takkyuubin (reading only) text:mntr:c2

Note: Although it is possible to enter only the name of a textbook or study list after the text: or list: key (e.g. text:cij or list:ap), this would show all kanji found in the respective resource. For this reason, it is usually helpful to append a volume and/or lesson number to limit your search (e.g. text:cij:c6 or list:ap:c3).

Please refer to Supported Textbooks and Study Lists on our website for additional details on the division of textbooks into volumes and/or chapters/lessons as well as textbook kanji not supported by Kanji alive.

Search Results

All kanji search results are initially shown in a single block, sorted by kanji stroke number. Two additional sort modes are available for viewing kanji, grouped together either by radical stroke number or kanji stroke number. In these two modes all kanji in a group are further sorted by the canonical order of their Japanese radicals. In the results, the radicals are shown in grey and in a smaller type size to the left of their respective kanji.

Navigation

Sort modes Click on the "Sort" icon in the navigation bar or use the up and down arrow keys to switch sort modes
Page results Click on the pointed brackets to the left and right of the "Sort" icon, drag the horizontal scrollbar or use the left and right arrow keys to view additional pages of results
View kanji details Click on a kanji to view its details
